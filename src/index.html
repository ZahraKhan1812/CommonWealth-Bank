Tips for Financial CyberSecurity

Simple CyberSecurity Guide for your Money

What You need to Know
Criminals want to steal your personal information and money through your computer and phone. They pretend to be banck or other trusted
companies to trick you into giving them your passwords, Scocial Security number, or bank detatis.

Warning Signs Someone May Have Hacked Your Accouut
  • Money missing from your accounts
  • Transactions you did not make
  • Account information changed without your permission
  • Missing bank statements or unexpected notifications
  • New Accounts or credit report that aren't yours
How to Protect Yourself

Keep Your Devices Safe
  • Turn on automatic updates for your computer, phone, and security
  • Never click links in suspicious emails or texts
  • Only download apps from official app stores
  • Use antivirus software on your computer
Create Strong Passwords
  • Passwords: Use different passwords for each account. Make them at least 10 characters with letters, numbers, and symbols. Don't use your name or birthday
  • Two-Factor Authentication: Turn this on wherever possible. It sends a code to your phone when you log in, making it much harder for criminals to break in.
Be Smart Online
  • Only enter personal information on secure websites (look for "https://" and a lock symbol in your browser)
  • Never give out passwords, Social Security numbers, or bank details in emails or phone calls
  • Log out completely when finished banking online
  • Avoid using public computer or Wi-Fi networks for banking
Stay Alert
  • Check your bank and credit card statements every month
  • Set up account alerts to notify you of any transactions
  • If something looks wrong, call your bank immiediately
What Real Companies Will Never Do
  • Ask for your password in an email or text
  • Request your Social Security number over email
  • Call demanding immediate action to "save" your account
If You Think You've Been Hacked
  1. Change your passwords immediately
  2. Contact your bank or credit card companies
  3. Check your credit report for any new accounts
  4. Report identiy theft at IdentityTheft.gov
Remember: When in doubt, hang up the phone or delete the email. Then contact your bank directly using the number on your statement or their official website.
